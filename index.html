  • Created wireframes, mockups, and interactive flows for multiple game modes and menu systems.
  • Collaborated with gameplay designers and developers to ensure interface consistency and responsiveness across platforms.
  • Participated in cross-platform UX optimization, ensuring visual clarity and input coherence between touch, controller, and remote navigation.

2022 – 2024 UX/UI Designer French Ministry of Economy and Finances Led and collaborated on internal digital projects to enhance public services. Designed user interfaces and improved user journeys in partnership with developers and data scientists, focusing on accessibility and clarity for a wide audience.
2019 – 2021 UX/UI & Web Designer Fullsix Worked across digital campaigns and platforms for major clients including Disney, Royal Canin, RATP, and Disneyland Paris. Designed responsive web interfaces, participated in UX research, and contributed to brand-consistent digital experiences.
2015 Junior UX/UI Designer Ubisoft Paris Supported UX and UI development for Far Cry Primal and Tom Clancy's The Division, collaborating closely with senior designers and developers.
  • Assisted in interface layout design, usability testing, and wireframing gameplay systems.
  • Learned and applied AAA production pipelines, including version control, documentation, and design review workflows.
  • Strengthened ability to translate complex gameplay data into intuitive user flows and clear player feedback systems.
2020 – 2024 UX/UI · Game · Narrative · 3D Designer Gobelins Paris Independent & academic shipped projects.
  • Aden — First-person parkour game prototype (Bachelor project). Designed and implemented core interface elements (HUD, menus, interaction prompts) focusing on player flow, diegetic feedback, and motion clarity.
  • Journal from the Abyss (Global Game Jam) — Designed UI mockups and UX flow for an experimental puzzle game, collaborating with programmers and artists to ship a playable prototype within 48 hours. Focused on readability, challenge pacing, and a minimalist aesthetic to reinforce player problem-solving immersion.
  • Deep Dive — Interactive exhibition exploring the experience of olympic swimmers through motion detection technology that reflects the user's steps to audio and visual feedback through different phases of the competition, using TouchDesigner.
